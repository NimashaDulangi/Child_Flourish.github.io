Project Scope

Literature Survey

Early detection of ASD and ADHD remains a challenge in Sri Lanka's healthcare landscape.

Figure 1. Estimated Distribution of Neurodevelopmental Profiles in Children.

This research explores an AI-powered early screening tool for Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), integrating psychological, cognitive, and emotional assessments. Leveraging deep learning models like CNNs and NLP techniques, the system evaluates emotional flat affect via voice analysis and face emotion detection.

This multimodal approach offers a scalable and objective alternative to traditional diagnostic methods. By combining machine learning with DSM-5 criteria, the system aims to enhance early detection accuracy and promote timely interventions [4].

Traditional assessments for ASD and ADHD often suffer from subjectivity and limited scalability. This study adopts AI and machine learning to enable a comprehensive, data-driven diagnostic framework. Emotional responsiveness is measured through voice features such as jitter, pitch, and prosody. ADHD likelihood is determined using psychological evaluations that factor in self-efficacy, depression, and anxiety levels [5]. Supported by recent studies in affective computing and NLP, this integrative platform provides a reliable and efficient solution for early neurodevelopmental screening.

[5] F. Ghassemi, M. H. Moradi, M. T. Doust, and V. Abootalebi, "Extracting principal components and determining critical questions in Conners ADHD questionnaire," in 2009 ICME International Conference on Complex Medical Engineering, Tempe, AZ, USA, 2009.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Absence of Parental Input of Child's Behavior

Most existing systems overlook parent-provided behavioral surveys, which are essential for capturing context-specific insights into a child's daily behavior. Without this input, diagnostic systems risk providing an incomplete picture of the child's development.

Lack of Attention Monitoring via Computer Vision

Despite the rise of computer vision, current tools fail to use video-based alertness tracking. Monitoring visual attention offers real-time, objective indicators of engagement—especially useful for identifying ASD symptoms—but is missing in prior work.

Ignoring Educational Response and Academic Sensitivity

Academic performance and sensitivity to educational content are often early signs of learning difficulties. However, prior research rarely considers these factors, reducing the scope of diagnosis and missing key indicators of developmental issues.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

Parents often struggle to balance work with their children's early education and development, especially for kids aged 8 to 11, a crucial period for social, emotional, and cognitive growth. During these formative years, children develop foundational skills in communication, emotional regulation, and problem-solving that will impact their lifelong learning and well-being. However, early signs of neurodevelopmental conditions such as Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) can be subtle and easily overlooked. Without timely identification and support, children may face challenges in academic achievement, peer relationships, and emotional health.

Many parents and educators lack access to effective, user-friendly tools for early detection and intervention. Traditional assessments can be time-consuming, expensive, or inaccessible, and may not capture the full range of a child's abilities and needs. This gap can delay critical support, impacting the child's confidence, learning trajectory, and social integration.

Proposed Solution

ChildFlourish offers an integrated, AI-powered platform designed specifically for children aged 8 to 11—a period recognized as vital for social, emotional, and cognitive development. Our solution combines voice analysis, facial reaction monitoring, cognitive assessments, and parent questionnaires to provide a holistic view of each child's development.

By leveraging advanced machine learning and emotional intelligence technologies, ChildFlourish can identify early indicators of ASD and ADHD, as well as support general cognitive and emotional growth. The platform empowers parents and educators with actionable insights, enabling timely interventions and personalized learning strategies. Our user-friendly interface ensures accessibility for families and schools, while maintaining privacy and ethical standards.

Through early detection and tailored support, ChildFlourish helps children thrive academically, socially, and emotionally—laying the foundation for lifelong success and well-being.

Research Objectives

Interactive Response System for ASD Detection

A user-friendly system will be created to capture children's emotional and verbal responses through voice prompts and facial expressions. It will monitor real-time facial cues along with the tone and clarity of their speech. The collected data will support the early identification of behavioral and communication patterns commonly linked to Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

Computerized Cognitive Test Suite

A series of computerized tests will be created to assess children's cognitive development. These tests will evaluate skills such as comprehension, creativity, problem-solving, and numerical reasoning, providing insights into their cognitive maturation. The results will help identify potential developmental delays and support personalized intervention strategies.

Psychological Assessment for ADHD Indicators

Psychological evaluations will be integrated to assess self-efficacy, personality traits, and mental health factors. These metrics are crucial in identifying behavioral symptoms associated with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). The findings will aid in forming a holistic profile of each child to support accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment planning

Advanced Prediction System for Early Detection of ASD and ADHD

Deep learning and machine learning algorithms will be employed to analyze data extracted from voice responses, cognitive tests, and psychological evaluations, enabling accurate prediction of ASD and ADHD likelihood in children.This integrated system empowers professionals and parents to make informed decisions for early intervention and tailored support.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed system leverages AI-powered modules to deliver comprehensive screening for Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). The process begins with the Voice Emotion-Based ASD Flat Affect Recognition (StoryCraft), which captures user responses in story format, initiating various diagnostic tools.

Voice inputs are analyzed for tone, pause, intensity, and emotion using the facial Emotion-Based ASD Flat Affect Recognition (EmoGauge). Simultaneously, video analysis extracts emotional expressions from facial features, aiding in detecting affective responses.

The Automated Educational Assessment module evaluates mental competencies using quizzes, drawing, and reading tasks. Results are stored centrally and trigger further evaluation if necessary.

To assess ADHD, the LLM-based ADHD Probability Assignment module compiles results from psychological tests such as self-efficacy and personality evaluations. These are processed via language models for scoring and sentiment analysis.

Finally, all diagnostic data—emotional, cognitive, and psychological—are aggregated into specialized dashboards, offering a detailed developmental profile and supporting early intervention decisions.
